Why We Started Bundl

As students, we noticed how stressful and emotionally exhausting it can be for parents, especially parents of infants, to constantly worry about their baby's comfort, safety, and overall well-being throughout the day and night.

Many existing baby-monitoring and wellness products are expensive and out of reach for a lot of families. We want to help create a future where parents don't have to spend overwhelming amounts of money just to feel informed and reassured about their child's well-being.

Mission & Goals

We genuinely believe in what we're building, and in the need for it. Too many parents spend their nights wondering if their baby is okay. We want to change that, and we know it's possible.

Right now, we're actively progressing toward building our prototype. Our primary focus is securing the funds and support needed to bring Bundl to life. We're also developing our Kickstarter campaign, and every waitlist sign-up is a real step forward for us: proof that families want this, and that we should keep going.

We want to make parenting feel more human, more informed, and more connected. Bundl isn't just a product; it's a journey we're building in real time, and we're grateful to have people alongside us for it.

Contributions go toward four main areas: (1) refining and testing our hardware prototype, (2) sourcing safe, baby-grade materials for the band, (3) safety validation and compliance testing with medical advisors, and (4) preparing our Kickstarter campaign so we can reach a wider audience and fund full production.

Bundl is a student-led startup founded by four students from Liberty Hill, Texas: Roopali Rahul (CEO), Meghana Malkareddy (CMO), Floristan Schwindt (CFO), and Samantha Fraunhofer (COO). We started this because we believe peace of mind for parents should not be a luxury.

Bundl is a smart wearable ankle band designed for infants that monitors key vitals, including heart rate, blood oxygen (SpO₂), skin temperature, motion, and sleep stage, continuously and sends real-time alerts to a parent's phone. We're currently in the prototype phase, working toward a Kickstarter launch.
